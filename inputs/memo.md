# Investment Memo — Paleo

**Date:** April 2026
**Round:** Pre-Seed — €300,000
**Pre-money valuation:** €1,500,000
**Instrument:** SAFE / Convertible note (negotiable)

---

## Executive summary

Paleo is an all-in-one management SaaS for independent restaurants in Spain. It solves the operational management problem that causes 74% of independent restaurants to close within 5 years.

In 6 months we have acquired 12 pilot restaurants with €2,400 MRR and 0% churn. We are raising €300K to scale our sales and technology team and reach 100 active restaurants in 18 months.

---

## The problem

Independent restaurants in Spain (300,000 locations) operate on 3-8% margins. Every optimization point in inventory, labor costs, or reservation management can be the difference between surviving and closing.

The problem isn't a lack of solutions — it's that existing solutions are designed for chains or solve only part of the problem (reservations OR POS OR inventory, never all three).

---

## Why we win

1. **All-in-one solution at an accessible price:** no competitor offers inventory + POS + reservations + cost analytics for under €300/month. We do it for €149.
2. **Team advantage:** María knows the pain from the inside (6 years operating restaurants). Carlos knows the tech infrastructure (5 years at Glovo integrating restaurants). It's the most obvious team to solve this problem.
3. **Defensible distribution:** partnerships with POS distributors give us access to their existing customer portfolio — a channel that SaaS competitors don't have.

---

## Investment thesis

The restaurant software market in Spain is worth €450M in the accessible segment and grows at 12% annually. If Paleo reaches 2% of SAM in 5 years, that's €9M ARR — sufficient for a >€50M exit with current SaaS multiples (5-8x ARR).

The team has an unfair advantage (domain + tech), validated early traction, and a distribution model that scales. The timing is right: post-COVID the sector is digitalized and looking to consolidate tools.

---

## Main risks

1. **Churn at scale:** the current 0% is exceptional. The real risk is that at 50+ customers the product isn't robust enough to retain.
   *Mitigation:* €60K of the budget goes to product robustness before accelerating sales.

2. **Well-funded competitor:** if a player like Lightspeed or Toast enters Spain aggressively, they could compress margins.
   *Mitigation:* our advantage is localization and price. We're faster at responding to the Spanish market.

3. **Sales velocity:** direct outbound is slow to scale.
   *Mitigation:* the partnership with POS distributors (under negotiation) can multiply the channel without high marginal cost.
