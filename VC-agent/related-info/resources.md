## Similar repos found

### 1. rk-vashista/pitch
**URL:** https://github.com/rk-vashista/pitch
**Description:** Pitch deck analysis web app with multi-agent architecture (CrewAI + FastAPI + GPT-4).

**Agents:**
- Pitch Analyzer — clarity, fundability
- Market Researcher — TAM/SAM/SOM (India-focused)
- Financial Analyst — unit economics, burn rate
- Website & Social Media Analyst — digital presence
- Investment Strategist — investment thesis, exits
- Due Diligence Analyst — risks

**Stack:** FastAPI, CrewAI, GPT-4 Turbo, WebSocket, Tailwind CSS

**Differences from preseed-lab:**
- Requires a server (not clonable and runnable without infra)
- Uses OpenAI, not Claude
- India-focused
- No feedback profiles
- Not specifically focused on Pre-Seed

## Differential advantage of preseed-lab

- **No server required** — runs directly from Claude Code, no infra
- **Clonable in 1 minute** — no complex installation
- **Pre-Seed focused** — specific stage with its own criteria
- **Feedback profiles** — Friendly Mentor / Brutal VC / Operator
- **Document-agnostic** — accepts PDF, DOCX, XLSX, MD
- **Open source** — any founder can adapt it


## Books

### In English

**Venture Deals** — Brad Feld & Jason Mendelson
https://www.amazon.com/Venture-Deals-Smarter-Lawyer-Capitalist/dp/1119594820
> The reference book for understanding term sheets, cap tables, and how to negotiate with investors. Essential reading.

**Secrets of Sand Hill Road** — Scott Kupor (Andreessen Horowitz)
https://www.amazon.com/Secrets-Sand-Hill-Road-Venture/dp/059308358X
> Explains how VCs think and make decisions. Written by the COO of a16z.

**The Holloway Guide to Raising Venture Capital** — Andy Sparks & Rachel Jepsen
https://www.holloway.com/g/venture-capital
> Comprehensive and free online guide to the entire fundraising process from the founder's perspective.

**Zero to One** — Peter Thiel
https://www.amazon.com/Zero-One-Notes-Startups-Future/dp/0804139296
> How to build something radically new. A classic on how the best investors think about a startup's potential.

**The Hard Thing About Hard Things** — Ben Horowitz
https://www.amazon.com/Hard-Thing-About-Things-Building/dp/0062273205
> Real lessons from building and scaling startups. Helps understand what VCs look for beyond the numbers.

---

### In Spanish

**Rondas de inversión: Método para financiar el crecimiento de tu startup** — Francisco Javier del Toro
https://www.amazon.es/Rondas-inversi%C3%B3n-financiar-crecimiento-startup/dp/8418372842

## Blogs & Online resources

### General reference (English)

**First Round Review**
https://review.firstround.com
> First Round Capital's blog. Deep articles on fundraising, product, and leadership for early-stage founders. One of the best.

**Y Combinator — Startup Library**
https://www.ycombinator.com/library
> Free YC library with guides on fundraising, pitch, unit economics, and growth. Essential.

**Paul Graham — Essays**
https://paulgraham.com/articles.html
> Reference essays on startups, investment, and how the best VCs think. "How to raise money" is required reading.

**a16z — Blog**
https://a16z.com/articles
> Andreessen Horowitz perspectives on markets, trends, and how they evaluate investments.

**Not Boring — Packy McCormick**
https://www.notboring.co
> Newsletter on startups and business strategy. Very useful for understanding how to narrate a startup to investors.
