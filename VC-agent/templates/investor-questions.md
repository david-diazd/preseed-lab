# Investor Question Bank — Pre-Seed and Seed

## How to use this document

This question bank is designed to help you prepare thoroughly before any investor meeting. Use it as follows:

- **Before a meeting**: Read all the questions and make sure you have a clear, concise answer for each. If you can't answer any with confidence, that's a signal you have work to do.
- **Practice out loud**: Knowing the answer in your head isn't enough. Say it out loud until it flows naturally and confidently.
- **Use the Q&A Simulator**: Load this file along with your information in the `vc-qa-simulator.md` agent to simulate a real Q&A session.
- **Identify your weak spots**: The questions that make you most uncomfortable are the ones you need to work on most. An investor will find them.
- **Update your answers regularly**: As your startup evolves, your answers should evolve with it.

> **Golden rule**: If a question catches you off guard in a real meeting, never improvise it again. Prepare the answer that same night.

## 1. Team

- **"Why you? What makes you the right people to solve this specific problem?"** — *(evaluates: whether a real unfair advantage exists based on experience, access, or domain knowledge)*

- **"When did you meet and how did you decide to start this together?"** — *(evaluates: strength of the co-founder relationship and whether the team formed out of conviction or convenience)*

- **"Have you worked together on something that went wrong before? How did you handle it?"** — *(evaluates: team resilience under conflict and adversity)*

- **"What does each co-founder do and how do you split decision-making responsibilities?"** — *(evaluates: clarity of roles and whether there is a real CEO with decision authority)*

- **"What is the equity split between co-founders and why was it done that way?"** — *(evaluates: whether the split reflects real contribution and whether it could create future conflicts)*

- **"Do all co-founders have vesting? With a cliff?"** — *(evaluates: whether the team has taken basic precautions to protect the company in the event of a co-founder departure)*

- **"What critical skill is the team missing right now and how do you plan to cover it?"** — *(evaluates: team self-awareness about their gaps and their plan to address them)*

- **"What is the entrepreneurial track record of each co-founder? Have they built something before?"** — *(evaluates: demonstrated execution ability and risk tolerance)*

- **"Does anyone on the team have work experience or a network in the sector you're attacking?"** — *(evaluates: market access, ability to get the first customers, and sector credibility)*

- **"If I call your last boss or business partner, what will they say about you?"** — *(evaluates: founder honesty and their real professional reputation)*

- **"Are you all full-time? If not, when will you be?"** — *(evaluates: real level of commitment to the project and whether there are signs someone lacks full conviction)*

- **"How long have you been working on this and what have you achieved since?"** — *(evaluates: execution speed and efficiency with available resources)*

## 2. Problem and Market

- **"When was the last time you spoke to a potential customer and what exactly did they tell you?"** — *(evaluates: whether the founder has real and ongoing contact with the market or works based on assumptions)*

- **"How do you know this problem is large enough to build a company on?"** — *(evaluates: rigor in problem validation and whether the founder has real evidence or just intuition)*

- **"Does the problem you solve exist today or is it one that will appear in the future? How do you know?"** — *(evaluates: market timing and whether the founder is ahead of the curve, perfectly timed, or late)*

- **"Who suffers this problem most? Who is paying to solve it right now, even with imperfect solutions?"** — *(evaluates: whether the founder knows precisely who their early adopter is and whether real demand already exists)*

- **"What is your TAM, SAM, and SOM? How did you arrive at those figures?"** — *(evaluates: rigor in market analysis and ability to build credible bottom-up estimates)*

- **"Is this a growing or stagnant market? What force is driving that growth?"** — *(evaluates: whether the wind is at their back or against them, and whether the founder understands macro trends)*

- **"Is there a reason this market hasn't been solved before? What has changed now?"** — *(evaluates: whether the founder can articulate the timing insight that makes their solution possible today)*

- **"How many potential customers have you interviewed and how many would pay for your solution today?"** — *(evaluates: depth of market research and whether real demand pull exists)*

- **"Is the problem you solve a vitamin or a painkiller? Why?"** — *(evaluates: real urgency of the problem and whether the customer would buy even with a tight budget)*

- **"Where is the money in this market right now? Where will it be in five years?"** — *(evaluates: sophistication of sector analysis and long-term strategic vision)*

- **"Are there regulations or legal barriers in this market that could affect your growth?"** — *(evaluates: awareness of external risks and whether the founder has done basic legal due diligence)*

## 3. Product and Solution

- **"Can you show me the product right now, live?"** — *(evaluates: whether the product actually exists and what the real experience is vs. what's shown in slides)*

- **"What is the non-obvious insight your solution is based on? What do you know that others don't?"** — *(evaluates: whether a real Thielian secret exists and whether the solution has intellectual defensibility)*

- **"Why is your solution 10x better than what the customer does today, not just a little better?"** — *(evaluates: real magnitude of the improvement and whether it's sufficient to change established behaviors)*

- **"How long does it take a new user to get the first real value from your product?"** — *(evaluates: time-to-value and adoption friction, especially critical at early stages)*

- **"What is the feature your users couldn't live without? How do you know?"** — *(evaluates: clarity about the core value and whether the founder knows what retains their users)*

- **"What part of the product is proprietary or hard to replicate for a competitor with more resources?"** — *(evaluates: technical, data, or network moat and long-term defensibility)*

- **"What have you removed from the product that you initially thought was important?"** — *(evaluates: prioritization ability, iterative learning, and real user orientation)*

- **"What is the product roadmap for the next 12 months and how is it tied to the business strategy?"** — *(evaluates: coherence between product vision and business objectives)*

- **"How much of your current product was built based on customer feedback vs. your own hypotheses?"** — *(evaluates: whether the founder builds based on evidence or on their own beliefs)*

## 4. Traction and Metrics

- **"Give me the three most important numbers in your business right now. Why those three?"** — *(evaluates: whether the founder has clarity on which metrics matter and whether they track them closely)*

- **"What is your retention rate at 30, 60, and 90 days?"** — *(evaluates: whether the product retains real users or whether there's a churn problem that growth metrics are masking)*

- **"How many active customers do you have today? Three months ago? Six months ago?"** — *(evaluates: real growth velocity and trend, not just the current absolute number)*

- **"How many of your current customers came from referrals or word of mouth?"** — *(evaluates: whether there is incipient product-market fit and whether the implicit NPS is high enough)*

- **"What is your NPS? How do you measure it?"** — *(evaluates: real customer satisfaction and measurement methodology)*

- **"How much revenue do you have? How much is recurring?"** — *(evaluates: evidence that customers actually pay and revenue structure)*

- **"What is your MoM growth rate over the last six months? Is it constant, accelerating, or decelerating?"** — *(evaluates: real business momentum and whether growth is sustainable)*

- **"What has been your biggest growth spike? What caused it?"** — *(evaluates: the founder's ability to understand growth drivers and whether they can replicate them)*

- **"What metrics have you stopped looking at because they were telling you things you didn't want to hear?"** — *(evaluates: founder honesty and maturity regarding negative business signals)*

- **"What is your current CAC and how has it evolved over the last three months?"** — *(evaluates: acquisition efficiency and whether the business is becoming more or less expensive to scale)*

- **"Do you have any enterprise or institutional customers validating the product? Which ones?"** — *(evaluates: whether reference customer validation exists that reduces the perceived risk for the investor)*

## 5. Business Model and Financials

- **"How exactly do you make money? What is your price and how did you arrive at it?"** — *(evaluates: business model clarity and rigor in pricing strategy)*

- **"What is your current LTV and how do you calculate it?"** — *(evaluates: understanding of the real value of each customer and the soundness of the assumptions in the calculation)*

- **"What is your LTV/CAC ratio? When do you expect it to exceed 3x?"** — *(evaluates: economic viability of the model and whether the founder knows when the business will be profitable per unit)*

- **"What is your gross margin? How does it evolve with scale?"** — *(evaluates: cost structure and whether the business improves or worsens economically as it grows)*

- **"How much cash are you burning per month right now?"** — *(evaluates: financial discipline and whether the founder has real control over spending)*

- **"How much runway do you have left with the money you have now?"** — *(evaluates: real urgency of the round and whether there's enough time to close it with margin)*

- **"When do you project reaching breakeven? What assumptions are behind that projection?"** — *(evaluates: financial realism and whether the founder understands the levers that control profitability)*

- **"What happens to your business model if CAC doubles? Does it still work?"** — *(evaluates: model robustness under adverse scenarios and risk management capacity)*

- **"Do you have debt right now? What type and on what terms?"** — *(evaluates: balance sheet structure and whether there are obligations that could complicate the investment)*

- **"What is your plan if you close this round at half of what you're asking for?"** — *(evaluates: plan adaptability and whether the founder has alternative scenarios prepared)*

- **"Give me your three largest cost lines right now and your plan for each."** — *(evaluates: deep knowledge of cost structure and financial discipline)*

## 6. Competition

- **"Who is your biggest competitor today? Not the most famous — the one that worries you most."** — *(evaluates: honesty about the real competitive landscape and whether the founder has done serious research)*

- **"If Google, Amazon, or Salesforce launched exactly what you're doing tomorrow, what would you do?"** — *(evaluates: ability to articulate the real moat and defense strategy against large-player threats)*

- **"Why will customers who currently use [competitor X] switch to you?"** — *(evaluates: understanding of switching costs and the differential value proposition in concrete terms)*

- **"What does your strongest competitor do better than you right now?"** — *(evaluates: honesty and self-awareness about real competitive weaknesses)*

- **"How do you know nobody is building exactly the same thing in a garage right now?"** — *(evaluates: ecosystem knowledge and ability to do continuous competitive intelligence)*

- **"What is your sustainable competitive advantage in 3 years? Why can't it be copied?"** — *(evaluates: strategic vision on how the moat builds over time and growth)*

- **"Have you spoken with your competitors' customers? What did they tell you about why they use them?"** — *(evaluates: depth of competitive analysis and whether the founder has real primary data)*

## 7. The Round and Use of Capital

- **"How much are you raising and at what valuation? How did you arrive at those numbers?"** — *(evaluates: rationality of the valuation and whether the founder can defend it with logic and data)*

- **"How exactly are you going to use the money from this round? Give me the breakdown."** — *(evaluates: clarity of the capital use plan and whether spending priorities are well justified)*

- **"What specific milestones do you expect to reach with this capital before raising the next round?"** — *(evaluates: whether the founder is clear on what they need to demonstrate to be fundable at Series A, and whether the milestones are realistic)*

- **"What kind of investor are you looking for beyond the money? What value-add do you need?"** — *(evaluates: founder sophistication in building their cap table and whether they know exactly what kind of support they need)*

- **"How much have the co-founders invested from their own money so far?"** — *(evaluates: founders' level of skin in the game and their real conviction in the project)*

- **"Have you spoken with other investors? What is the status of those conversations?"** — *(evaluates: whether real competition exists in the round and how advanced the fundraising process is)*

- **"If you don't close this round in the next 90 days, what happens? Do you have a plan B?"** — *(evaluates: resilience and ability of the business to survive without this capital injection)*

## 8. Trap Questions and Hard Ones

These are the questions investors ask specifically to see how you react. They're not looking for the perfect answer: they're looking for honesty, mental agility, and maturity. The worst answers are defensive, evasive, or ones that try to sell instead of explain.

- **"What has been your biggest mistake so far? What did you learn?"** — *(evaluates: humility, learning capacity, and whether the founder has the maturity to acknowledge mistakes without going into defensive mode)*

- **"Why haven't you grown faster? What has held you back?"** — *(evaluates: self-awareness about real business bottlenecks and whether the founder can be honest about their limitations)*

- **"If I gave you the money today, what would you do differently from what you've done so far?"** — *(evaluates: whether the founder has learned from their experience and has clarity on what they would change with more resources)*

- **"What if the market doesn't grow as fast as you project? Is it still a good business?"** — *(evaluates: robustness of the business thesis under conservative scenarios and whether the founder has done sensitivity analysis)*

- **"Why should I invest in you and not in the team building exactly the same thing but with more traction?"** — *(evaluates: ability to articulate the differential directly under pressure and without getting defensive)*

- **"Have you thought about pivoting? Is there something about the current business that deep down you're not convinced by?"** — *(evaluates: honesty about internal doubts and whether the founder has real conviction or is pursuing something by inertia)*

- **"When was the last time an important customer left the product? Why did they leave?"** — *(evaluates: whether the founder knows the reasons for churn and whether they have a system for learning from lost customers)*

- **"If in two years this hasn't worked out as you hoped, what will have gone wrong?"** — *(evaluates: ability to anticipate real business risks and whether the founder has honestly thought through failure scenarios)*

- **"Are you willing to relocate or change the business model if the investor considers it necessary?"** — *(evaluates: founder flexibility and whether there is alignment of expectations about post-investment control)*
