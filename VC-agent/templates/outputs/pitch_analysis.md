# Analysis: Pitch Deck Analyst
**Startup:** Paleo
**Date:** April 2026
**Feedback profile:** Brutal VC

---

## Diagnosis

Paleo's pitch deck has a competent structure and the real numbers are honest, which is appreciated. The problem is that the deck recounts facts but doesn't build a narrative that creates urgency. An investor reading it without prior context will understand what Paleo does, but won't feel compelled to get in now. The market opportunity is underexploited in the presentation: 300K independent restaurants in Spain is a powerful number that the deck mentions in passing. The team is solid on paper, but the founders slide doesn't convey why they are the ones who are going to win this market and not another team with similar CVs.

## Main problems

1. **Problem narrative too generic** — The problem slide says "restaurants manage everything with fragmented tools." That's true, but any competitor could say the same. What's missing is a concrete story: a real restaurant, a real loss, a number that hurts (how many hours per week does a restaurant manager waste, how much money leaks from inefficiencies).

2. **The competition section is defensive, not offensive** — The competition map shows that Covermanager, Agora POS, and TheFork don't cover the all-in-one. Correct. But the deck doesn't explain why none of them can add those features. Without a clear barrier, the investor thinks: "What stops Agora POS from launching a reservations module tomorrow?"

3. **Traction is buried** — 12 pilot restaurants with 0% churn in 3 months is the most powerful data point in the deck. It appears in a dense metrics slide, without visual prominence. That's a serious mistake. Zero churn in B2B SaaS at this stage is the strongest signal you can give an investor.

4. **Use of funds is vague** — "Marketing, product, and team" is not an investment plan. A Pre-Seed investor wants to know: with €300K, how many new customers will you acquire? What MRR milestone do you hit to set up the next round? What exactly are you hiring?

5. **The product slide doesn't show the product** — There are static screenshots. In 2026, a SaaS deck should include at least a GIF or an interactive demo link. Investors want to see the real flow, not polished mockups.

6. **Long-term vision is absent** — The deck covers the here and now well. But there's no slide that answers: where is Paleo going in 5 years? Is it just Spain? Just restaurants? What's the path to €10M ARR? Pre-Seed investors know the path will change, but they need to see that the founders think big.

## Recommendations

1. **Rewrite the problem slide with a story** — Open the deck with the daily reality of María, a restaurant manager in Madrid who uses 6 different tools and loses 12 hours per week on administration. Give her a name, a face, a concrete economic cost. That creates emotional connection before getting into data.

2. **Build an explicit moat argument** — Add a dedicated slide explaining why existing competitors can't solve this: they are trapped in their current business model (TheFork charges per reservation, can't switch to SaaS), or they have technical debt that prevents them from integrating new verticals. You need the investor to understand why the window is open and why it's going to close.

3. **Put zero churn on the cover or executive summary** — "0% churn in 3 months with 12 pilot customers" deserves to be in the first 3 slides. Consider adding a literal customer quote next to the data point. Retention is your strongest argument right now.

4. **Replace the use of funds with a milestone plan** — Structure it like this: €300K → 12 months → 80 customers → €12K MRR → positioned to raise Seed of €1M. Every spending line must connect directly to a measurable milestone that unlocks the next round.

5. **Add a demo link or product GIF** — Record a 90-second Loom showing the main flow (reservation management + end-of-day closing + weekly analytics). Embed it in the deck as a QR or link. The best early-stage SaaS decks show real product.

## Readiness score
**6/10**

The deck correctly communicates what Paleo does and has honest real metrics, which already puts it ahead of 60% of the decks seen at this stage. However, the narrative doesn't create urgency, the zero churn isn't highlighted as it deserves, and the use of funds doesn't connect to concrete milestones. With two focused iteration rounds on storytelling and milestone structure, this deck can reach 8/10 before the first investor meetings.
