# Analysis: VC Partner
**Startup:** Paleo
**Date:** April 2026
**Feedback profile:** Brutal VC

---

## Diagnosis

Paleo has the basic ingredients I look for in a Pre-Seed: a complementary team with direct operational experience in the sector, a real and well-defined problem, and initial retention metrics pointing in the right direction. María González understands the customer's pain because she lived it for 6 years, and Carlos Ruiz knows how to build product for critical operations from Glovo. That's not trivial. The problem is they're asking for €300K at a €1.5M pre-money valuation with 12 customers, €2,400 MRR, and 4 months of runway. The valuation is reasonable in absolute terms, but the execution risk remains very high: there's no proven acquisition channel beyond the founder's manual outbound, and the independent restaurant market has a long history of solutions that fail to scale beyond the first enthusiastic customers. I need more evidence before committing capital.

## Main problems

1. **No proven scalable distribution channel** — 100% of the 12 current customers came from the CEO's direct outbound. That's consistent with pre-seed stage, but it's also the biggest risk in the business. Paleo's problem isn't whether the product works — zero churn suggests it does — it's whether they can acquire customers efficiently when the CEO stops doing in-person sales. Without evidence of a channel that scales (partnerships, inbound, referrals), growth is entirely tied to hiring salespeople, which is costly and uncertain.

2. **Long-term defensibility isn't clear** — In restaurant software, switching barriers exist (historical data, team training), but they're not insurmountable. A well-funded competitor — or an Agora POS that decides to launch reservations or a Covermanager that adds a management module — can replicate the features in 12-18 months. I don't see in the pitch a moat that can't be copied with enough capital. I need to understand what makes Paleo a business that money alone can't replicate.

3. **The path to fund return isn't obvious from this valuation** — At €1.5M pre-money, for a 30x return on my investment (standard VC seed fund), Paleo needs to reach a valuation of €45M-€50M in 5-7 years. That implies €8M-€10M ARR with reasonable multiples, or a strategic exit to a major sector player (POS, hospitality ERP, marketplace). What's the most likely path? I don't see it articulated in the pitch.

4. **The 4-month runway creates artificial urgency** — I don't make good investment decisions when a founder is negotiating with water at their neck. With 4 months of runway, any serious due diligence consumes half that time. That pressures me to make a decision faster than I'd like, and that's not good for either party.

5. **Lack of evidence of product referrability** — 0% churn is a positive signal, but I want to know whether those 12 restaurants would actively recommend Paleo. How many referrals have they generated? Has any restaurant brought in another from the same group or the same neighborhood? Referrability in hospitality is the best indicator of real product-market fit in this segment.

## Recommendations

1. **Close 5-8 more customers before talking to VC investors** — Get to 20 customers before the next round of meetings. With 20 customers and €3K-€3,500 MRR, the conversation changes. You're no longer selling hypotheses, you're selling an adoption pattern.

2. **Test a second acquisition channel even if small** — Launch a referral program with an incentive (one month free per closed referral). Even if it generates 2-3 customers, it changes the narrative: you no longer depend solely on manual outbound.

3. **Articulate the path to exit or fund scale** — Prepare a slide or a 1-page document with return scenarios: base scenario (€5M ARR, exit to vertical ERP €40M), ambitious scenario (expansion to Portugal + Italy, €15M ARR, strategic exit €100M+). You don't need to convince me it will happen, you need to demonstrate it's mathematically possible.

4. **Find a lead investor with B2B vertical SaaS or hospitality expertise** — Capital isn't enough at this stage. You need an investor who has seen how this type of business is scaled and can open doors to the first 50-100 installations. An operational business angel in the sector is worth more than a generalist fund at this valuation.

## Investment verdict
**Need more information**

I like the team and the retention metrics are real. But I can't commit capital with 12 customers, a single unproven-at-scale acquisition channel, and a 4-month runway that pressures me to make a sub-optimal decision. What I need to say yes: see the product in a real 30-minute demo, speak directly with 2-3 of the pilot customers to validate retention, understand the current pipeline (how many demos are scheduled, how many restaurants are in the closing process), and receive a revised financial model with the real CAC including the sales team cost. If in 4-6 weeks they reach 18-20 customers while maintaining zero churn, the conversation is different.

## Readiness score
**6/10**

The fundamentals are there: right team, real problem, first signs of solid retention, and reasonable valuation. What's missing to reach 8/10 is more traction (more customers, more MRR), evidence of at least one secondary acquisition channel, and an articulated fund return narrative. Paleo is on the edge between "too early" and "just the right moment." The next month of execution is decisive.
