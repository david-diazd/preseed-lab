**Feedback profile:** Brutal VC

> **Methodological note:** This analysis simulates the output of an agent with web search capability. The referenced sources are representative examples of the type of information the agent would retrieve from LinkedIn, X/Twitter, Google, specialized media, and public databases.

## Diagnosis

Paleo's team is their most solid asset. María González brings exactly the type of operational credential that differentiates a founder who understands the problem from the inside — not from a consulting firm. Carlos Ruiz comes with real execution experience in infrastructure at scale, something very few vertical SaaS CTOs in Spain can demonstrate. The risk isn't in the individual quality of each founder, but in the complementarity: both have execution profiles, neither has a native commercial profile. In a Pre-Seed phase where founder-led sales is critical, that's a gap investors will attack in the first meeting.

The pain that Paleo solves — tool fragmentation, operational data loss, legacy system dependency — is exactly what she managed during that period. Source: verified LinkedIn profile (*linkedin.com/in/mariagonzalez-ops*, ~1,200 connections, management-level recommendations from the group).

- **Track record:** IESE MBA Barcelona, class of 2017 (top 5% per Financial Times 2024 ranking for Iberian programs). Before Meliá, two years as operations consultant at Deloitte Spain, Hospitality & Retail practice. No prior experience founding companies, which is neutral at Pre-Seed but becomes a risk at Series A if she hasn't demonstrated hiring and investor management capability.

- **Online presence:** Moderate. Active on LinkedIn (3-4 posts/month on hospitality and technology, average engagement ~200 interactions). Mentioned as a speaker at the Spain Hospitality Tech Summit 2025 ("Digitalization in independent restaurants: why the sector is 10 years behind," November 2025 — coverage in *Hosteltur.com*). X account with ~340 followers, low activity.

- **Notable signals:**
  - Positive: Featured in the *"30 Women Transforming Spanish Hospitality"* list in Restauración News magazine (February 2026). Direct contacts with sector decision-makers (independent groups, provincial hospitality associations). Real operational network, not theoretical.
  - Negative: No capital-raising track record. First time in the CEO role at her own company. No public thesis on the market beyond her 2025 talk — investors will want to see she has strong and public opinions about where the sector is going.

## Main problems

1. **No commercial profile in the founding team.** Neither founder has direct experience in B2B sales, pipeline building, or SaaS contract negotiation. María knows the sector as a customer and operator, not as a seller. At Pre-Seed, the CEO has to be the company's best salesperson. This will be the first question from any VC with experience in vertical SaaS.

2. **Carlos has never been the sole technical person responsible for an architecture from scratch.** Glovo has platform teams, SRE, DevOps. Paleo won't have that. The learning curve from "senior contributor at scale" to "founding architect at early stage" is real and underestimated.

3. **No advisor with investor track record on the team.** To close a Pre-Seed round in Spain in 2026 — with a more demanding market than in 2021-2022 — having someone who has founded and sold a company or has direct relationships with the target funds can make the difference between a 3-month round and a 9-month one.

---

## Recommendations

1. **María should publish a market thesis before the first investor meetings.** A 1,500-word article on LinkedIn about why software for independent restaurants is broken and what needs to change. Objective: have investors arrive at the meeting having read something from her, not discovering her in the moment.

2. **Bring in a Head of Sales as the key first hire or as an advisor with equity.** Someone with experience selling SaaS to SMEs in the Spanish hospitality sector (Agora, Revo, or similar companies are a direct pipeline). Not optional — it's the piece the team needs to be fundable on decent terms.

3. **Carlos should publicly document Paleo's architectural decisions.** A technical thread on LinkedIn or X about how they're solving integrations with the 5 main POS systems in the Spanish market is extremely valuable content that reinforces the fundraising technical narrative and attracts talent.

4. **Find an advisor with credibility in the Spanish VC ecosystem.** South Summit, Lanzadera, Ship2B — there's enough network in the Canaries and on the mainland to reach someone who has seen this type of deal before and can offer perspective on the target funds' deal flow.

## Readiness score

**8 / 10**

Paleo's team is the strongest argument in the deck. María has the real domain expertise and the sector network; Carlos has the scale technical credential. In a market where most hospitality SaaS founders have never operationally managed a restaurant for a single day, that's a structural advantage that's hard to manufacture. The 2/10 that's missing reflects three concrete gaps: no native commercial profile, Carlos's inexperience as a solo technical leader, and no advisor with investor traction. None of the three is fatal at Pre-Seed, but all three are questions that will come up in every due diligence. Show up with prepared answers or investors will assume you don't have them.
