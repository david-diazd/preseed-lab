# Analysis: Growth Expert
**Startup:** Paleo
**Date:** April 2026
**Feedback profile:** Brutal VC

---

## Diagnosis

Paleo has real traction but a fragile growth model. The 35% demo-to-customer conversion rate is excellent — the B2B SaaS average at this ticket size is 20-25% — and 0% churn over 3 months indicates the product solves a genuine problem. The fundamental problem is that all current growth depends on María González making cold calls, closing demos, and accompanying onboarding. That's not a growth engine: it's a founder working hard. When María needs to manage the company, fundraise, or scale the team, the pipeline stops. At €2,400 MRR with 12 customers after months of operation, the acquisition pace is too slow for the urgency imposed by the 4-month runway. The current GTM works to validate the product, but it's not designed to grow.

## Main problems

1. **Total dependency on founder manual outbound** — All 12 current customers have come through the same channel: the CEO's direct calls and visits. That means if María stops selling for 3 weeks (which will inevitably happen during the round), the pipeline empties. There's no lead generation mechanism that works without her: no inbound, no active referrals, no partnerships, no content. A business that only grows when the founder sells is a business that hasn't solved its distribution problem.

2. **12 customers over several months is an insufficient acquisition pace** — With a reasonable sales cycle of 2-3 weeks between first contact and signing, and a 35% demo conversion, Paleo should be able to close 4-6 customers per month with full-time sales dedication. The current pace suggests there are bottlenecks in prospecting (few demos generated), in the sales cycle (it drags longer than expected), or in onboarding (which consumes time that could go to new sales). Without knowing which is the bottleneck, you can't design the solution.

3. **No referral or community strategy** — The independent restaurant sector in Spain operates on trust and word of mouth. A restaurant that tries a new tool and it works tells other restaurants in the same neighborhood, guild, or association. Paleo has no formal mechanism to convert that natural dynamic into an acquisition channel. A simple referral program (one month free per referred restaurant that signs) could generate 30-40% of new customers at almost zero marginal cost.

4. **The 12-month growth plan assumes scaling outbound without validating the channel** — If the projection is to reach 80 customers in 12 months, that requires hiring salespeople. But the current sales playbook is in María's head, not in a replicable document. Without a documented sales process (exact ICP, prospecting sequence, pitch, common objections and responses), the first sales rep you hire will take 3 months to reach the CEO's pace, and with poor support, could tank your CAC.

5. **No content strategy or brand authority** — In the restaurant market, positioning as hospitality management experts is worth a lot. A blog with 10 articles on restaurant management, an F&B cost optimization guide, or a weekly newsletter for restaurant managers builds trust and generates inbound over time. None of that currently exists.

## Recommendations

1. **Document the sales process before hiring anyone** — This week: record the next 3 of María's demos on Loom, transcribe them, and extract the real pitch she uses. Create a 2-page document with the ICP (revenue, number of covers, current tools, buying signals), the prospecting sequence, and the 5 most common objections with their responses. That document is the foundation for the first sales rep and is worth more than any formal training.

2. **Launch a referral program in the next 2 weeks** — Call your 12 current customers and offer them one month free for every restaurant they refer that signs a contract. You don't need technology for this: a WhatsApp message and a spreadsheet are enough at this stage. Measure how many referrals each customer generates. If the product NPS is high (and zero churn suggests it is), this channel can become your second acquisition engine in 60 days.

3. **Identify and activate 2-3 distribution partnerships** — Look for provincial hospitality associations (Madrid, Barcelona, Valencia), kitchen equipment distributors, and restaurant insurance managers. These players have access to hundreds of restaurants and one conversation with them is worth more than 50 cold calls. Propose a simple partnership model: they recommend Paleo to their customer base and receive a recurring commission (10-15% of the first year of the contract).

4. **Measure and optimize the sales funnel with data** — Implement a basic CRM (HubSpot Free is enough) to track: leads generated per week, demos scheduled, demo-to-customer conversion rate, average sales cycle time, and reasons for loss. With 4 weeks of data, you'll know exactly where the bottleneck is and can attack it surgically before scaling.

## Readiness score
**5/10**

The initial traction is real and the 35% demo conversion is a positive sign that the product sells when shown. But the current growth model isn't an engine: it's a founder working. Without a second acquisition channel, without a documented sales process, and without funnel data, scaling to 80 customers in 12 months is going to require twice the effort and twice the burn projected. The good news is that the most obvious growth catalysts — referrals, guild partnerships, sales process documentation — are low-cost, high-impact actions that can be activated in the next 4-6 weeks. The potential is there; the system is still missing.
