# Template: Pre-Seed Pitch Deck

Recommended structure for a Pre-Seed pitch deck. Each section includes what to include and what to avoid.

---

## Slide 1 — Cover
**What to include:**
- Startup name and logo
- One-line tagline (what you do and for whom)
- Presenting founder's name
- Date

**Avoid:** generic slides with no clear tagline.

---

## Slide 2 — The problem
**What to include:**
- The specific pain you solve (in the customer's terms, not yours)
- Who suffers it (concrete profile)
- Why the problem exists now / why it hasn't been solved yet
- If you have a data point or real story: use it

**Avoid:** vague or overly broad problems. "Companies waste time" is not a problem.

---

## Slide 3 — The solution
**What to include:**
- Your solution in 1-2 sentences
- Screenshot or product demo (if it exists)
- What makes your solution different from what's out there

**Avoid:** describing features before value. Lead with the outcome for the customer, then how you achieve it.

---

## Slide 4 — Why now (timing)
**What to include:**
- What has changed in the world (technology, regulation, behavior) that makes your solution possible today
- Why it wasn't possible 3-5 years ago

**Note:** This slide separates good pitches from mediocre ones. If you don't have a clear answer, work on it.

---

## Slide 5 — Market (TAM/SAM/SOM)
**What to include:**
- TAM: total market (with bottom-up methodology, not just a number)
- SAM: market accessible to you in the next few years
- SOM: realistic target for 3-5 years
- Data sources

**Avoid:** large numbers with no justification. "The market is $500B" without context convinces no one.

---

## Slide 6 — Business model
**What to include:**
- How you make money (pricing, revenue model)
- Price per unit/customer
- Basic unit economics: CAC, LTV, LTV:CAC

**Avoid:** "future" business models with no clarity on how you monetize today.

---

## Slide 7 — Traction
**What to include:**
- Most important metrics with time frame ("from X to Y in Z weeks")
- Current customers, MRR, growth
- Customer logos if recognizable
- If there's no traction: leave this slide out

**Avoid:** vanity metrics (downloads without activation, users without revenue, "thousands of interested people").

---

## Slide 8 — Competition
**What to include:**
- Main direct and indirect competitors
- Honest comparison table (including where they're better)
- Your clear differential position

**Avoid:** saying "we have no competition" — it signals you don't know your market well.

---

## Slide 9 — Team
**What to include:**
- Founders with photo, name, and role
- 1-2 lines on why each is the right person for this
- Team's unfair advantage (domain, network, technology)
- Relevant advisors (if any)

**Avoid:** long CVs. What matters: why does this team win this market?

---

## Slide 10 — Go-to-market
**What to include:**
- Primary customer acquisition channel (validated or clear hypothesis)
- Plan for the next 12 months
- How you reach the first 100 customers

---

## Slide 11 — Financials
**What to include:**
- Simplified P&L: current revenue and 3-year projection
- Current monthly burn rate
- Current runway
- Key financial milestones

**Avoid:** projections with no basis. Show the assumptions behind the numbers.

---

## Slide 12 — The round
**What to include:**
- How much you're raising
- At what valuation (pre-money)
- Use of capital (% by category: team, product, marketing, ops)
- Milestone you'll reach with this round
- Post-round runway

---

## Final notes

- **Ideal length:** 10-14 slides. More than 15 signals you haven't prioritized.
- **Target reading time:** 3-5 minutes for the version sent by email.
- **Design:** clean and consistent. Content matters more than design, but a messy deck loses credibility.
- **Versions:** have two versions — one to send by email (complete) and one to present live (more visual, less text).
